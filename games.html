Games I'm Playing Right Now

Updated on February 2020

Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice

The game puts the player in the mindset of a Shinobi -- players need to react quickly to the enemy's arm movement, use different tactics to win, and practice over and over again. Starting out, the game, especially the Boss fights, are demanding and frustrating. But after beating the game three times, I fell in love with the combat systems and truly feel like a great Shinobi that can take on anything -- this is a feeling no other games have managed to give me.

This is a good game, but I like God of War better. God of War allows beginners to enjoy the game while allowing hardcore gammers to focuing on the combats. This game only allows the latter.

Platform: PS4

Dreams

Handing game making power to the players is a cool idea. I haven't tried "dreams making", instead I "dream surf" a lot -- playing games made by others. Even though most games are of low quality, but when you have a good quantity of them, it became a kaleidoscope that showcases people's unique ideas.

I do hope they can improve their recommendation algorism.

Games I've Played in 2020

Super Mario Odyssey

A simple game that is surprisingly fun to play. I played the game with friends at parties, played it single on airplanes. I never thought collecting coins can be so much fun.

Platform: Switch

Uncharted 4

The first few chapters are certainly fun to play. My heart rate will spike as I climb through the buildings. But as the game progress, the gameplay became repetitive.

Platform: PS4

Supraland

A fun and casual puzzle solving game! If you enjoyed the puzzles in Portal and the adventures in the Legend of Zelda, you should definitely try this game out!

Platform: PC

Ni No Kuni

I always love creative Japanese animated movies (think Hayao Miyazaki). This game successfully merges that with innovative real-time turn-based combat. It's worth trying for anyone who likes a good story.

Platform: Switch

Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice

The game that got everything right but gameplay. Hellblade has one of the most photorealistic graphics, immersive environment, and sound effects, plus satisfying combat.

But the puzzle-solving, which players spend half of their time doing, is boring, frustrating and luck-based. Player feedback is non-existing or confusing in this game. This game shows the importance of playtesting.

Platform: Xbox Game Pass

Wolfenstein II: The New Colossus

I prefer Call of Duty/MGV style FPS game, so for me Wolfenstein II isn't realistic enough. The feedback for loss of blood is also vague and based on a numeric systems. I died multiple times becuase I thought I was in full health when in fact I'm in 50% health. Nonetheless, I can see why other players would enjoy this game.

Platform: Xbox Game Pass

Rainbow Six Siege

The best online tactical shooter game I have ever played. The focus on tactical defense and insertion, environmental destruction and cooperation between teammates give the player a highly immersive experience.

Platform: PC
